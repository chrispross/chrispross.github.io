Electoral Reform: Weighted Voting Proposal

The Proposal

Keep FPTP for candidate selection, but weight MPs' votes in parliament by their party's vote share.

This visualization compares Canada's current First-Past-The-Post (FPTP) electoral system with a proposed weighted voting system across six recent federal elections (2008-2025).

Key Benefits

  • Retains local representation - Each riding still elects one MP
  • Filters extreme views - Candidates must win a plurality in a local riding
  • Achieves proportionality - Parliamentary voting power matches popular vote share
  • Simple to implement - No redistricting, no new ballots, just a formula for vote counting
